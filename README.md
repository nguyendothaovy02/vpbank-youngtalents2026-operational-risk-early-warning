## 1. What this project does

Operational risk in a digital bank is usually recorded **after** an incident -
once customers complain and losses are booked. This project prototypes an
**early-warning system** that detects operational stress **before** it becomes
a full incident, using:

1. **Six Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)** spanning system, process and people risk.
2. An **auditable SQL pipeline** that computes each KRI from operational logs.
3. An **unsupervised anomaly-detection model** (Isolation Forest) that flags
   abnormal *combinations* of KRIs, even when no single KRI has breached its
   static threshold.

The goal metric is **Time-to-Detect**: reducing mean detection time from a
reactive ~4 hours (customer-complaint driven) to a proactive ~30 minutes
(signal driven). Both figures are illustrative targets, not measured values.

---

## 2. The six KRIs

| # | KRI | Dimension | Warning threshold |
|---|-----|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Transaction Failure Rate (SYSTEM + PARTNER only) | System | > 5% |
| 2 | Failure Rate Velocity (rate of change) | System | > 2× |
| 3 | Users with ≥ 3 failed transactions | System → Customer | > 2% |
| 4 | eKYC step-level drop-off | Process | any step > μ + 2σ |
| 5 | Manual Override Rate | Process / People | > μ + 2σ |
| 6 | Overdue Mandatory Training | People | > 10% |

Thresholds follow a **baseline + 2σ (≈ p95)** rule computed on historical data,
reviewed quarterly. This is reproducible in the notebook, not hand-picked.

**Deliberately excluded:** System Downtime (a *lagging* indicator - it reports a
failure rather than warning of it) and staff turnover (measured on a monthly
cycle, unsuitable for real-time early warning).

computes the KRIs, sets the static thresholds, trains the Isolation Forest,
validates it, and saves `anomaly_detection_chart.png`.

## 5. Model validation - honest reading of the results

Because real incident labels are scarce, the model is validated by
**synthetic-anomaly injection**: `generate_data.py` plants three known SYSTEM
outages (2026-02-28, 2026-04-14, 2026-05-31). **Recall** = how many the model
recovers.

**Design target:** Recall > 90% at a low false-positive rate.

**Result on this synthetic dataset:** Recall = **100% (3/3)**.

This recall is high partly *by construction* - the injected outages are
deliberately pronounced (failure rate spikes to 8-10% vs a ~1.5% baseline),
so any reasonable detector should recover them. The point of the exercise is
not to celebrate 100%, but to confirm the end-to-end pipeline works and to
study the model's **false positives**.

**Error analysis.** The model also flagged three non-outage days. Inspection
shows these cluster around **pay-days**, where transaction volume and failures
rise for benign seasonal reasons. This is exactly why the model includes
**calendar features** (pay-day / Tet flags) so predictable spikes are not
treated as incidents, and why the design keeps a **human-in-the-loop** review
before any critical alert is escalated - to prevent alert fatigue.

## 6. Governance

- **Drift monitoring:** alarm on data-distribution shift (PSI).
- **Scheduled retraining:** monthly updates against fresh baselines.
- **Human-in-the-loop:** expert review required before any critical action.
- **Interpretability:** a simple model is chosen deliberately - every alert
  must be explainable to the 2nd line of defense.
